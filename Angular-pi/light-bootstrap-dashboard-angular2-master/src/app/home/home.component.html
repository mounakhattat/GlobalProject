SDGs

Microfinance institutions (MFIs) play a critical role in advancing the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) by providing access to financial services to low-income and underserved populations. By enabling people to save, borrow, and invest, MFIs contribute to poverty reduction, economic growth, and financial inclusion, which are key drivers of many of the SDGs.

Perform banking operations online

Microfinance institutions have been at the forefront of adopting and implementing digital financial services, including online banking. By leveraging technology, microfinance institutions can provide their clients with convenient and secure access to a range of financial products and services, such as savings accounts, loans, and money transfers. Online banking allows microfinance clients to manage their accounts, access information and services.

Microfinance

Microfinance helps to create jobs and generate income for people, especially those in rural areas or from marginalized communities. This, in turn, helps to promote economic growth and reduce poverty.Microfinance provides access to financial services to people who have limited or no access to traditional banking services, which can help to reduce poverty and promote economic development.
